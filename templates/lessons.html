We Keep some double sided index cards on hand for chefs new to the kitchen

-- Quality Knife!

  • - Use a 6 inch chef knife for all basic chopping/cutting/filleting.

  • - Use a cleaver if you are chopping tough veg. The added weight is helpful.

  • - Serrated knives are bread knives, NOT chef knives!

    • More on back -->

  • - Hone your knife with a honing rod before each meal prep.

  • - Sharpen your knives once a year or more depending on use.

  • - A dull tears while a shape knife slices.

-- Lazy Planning!

  • - A 10 minute marinade is still a marinade

  • - Fat Raccoon secret: Add a splash of water to your marinade

  • - Decide what a single portion means to you and own tupperware that accomodates that!

    • More on back -->

  • - Own quality tupperware. If you cannot freeze it, replace it!

  • - Everytime you cook, place a minimum of a single portion in the freezer

  • - Freeze your extra chicken stock

-- Diet Impacts Us!

  • - Eatting a balanced diet matters to overall health.

  • - Refined sugar and preservatives are hidden in restraunt and processed foods.

  • - Spice, heat, peppers improve taste and help compensate for less salt.

    • More on back -->

  • - Portion control is as important as balanced diet!

  • - Spice, soluble fiber, and drinking more water help you feel more full.

  • - A proper diet helps us feel happier, more motivated, and less depressed.

-- Personal Perspective

  • - Spending time in the kitchen can feel therapeutic, calming, and rewarding.

  • - Fat Raccoon secret: Put 'Worst Cooks in America" on while you are cooking. And remember we all started at the same place.

    • More on back -->

  • - Everyone's taste buds are different.

  • - Changing a recipe to accomodate for personal taste is important.

  • - Remember, cooking is a skill that imporves as you practice. Learn from your mistakes.

--Know Your Flavors!

  • - Bitter: Cirus Rind, Cocoa, Coffee, Red Wine, etc...

  • - Fats(Healthy oils): olive oil, peanut oil, sesame oil ,etc...

  • - Salt: Soy Sause, Bacon, Chili Paste, Anchovies, etc...

    • More on back -->

  • - Spice(heat): chipotle(mature jalapeno), ancho(poblano), sambal, black/white pepper, etc...

  • - Sweet: Fruit, caramelized veg, carrots, etc...

  • - Sour(acidity): Citrus, Pickled Veg, Vinegar, Yogurt, etc...

  • - Umami(Amino Acid and glutamate): Fish, cured meats, mushrooms, ripe tomato, soy sauce, etc...

--Know Your Flavor pairs!

  • - Sour(acidity) pairs well with fat(oil) or sweetness.

  • - Bitter pairs well with sweet and fat(oil) flavors.

  • - Spice(heat) pairs well with fat(oil), sweet, and sour.

    • More on back -->

  • - Sweet pairs well with acidity because its complimented by acidity's sourness.

  • - Salt pairs well with fat(oil) and compliments sweetness.

  • - Fat is flavor! Healthy fats(oils) bring a lot to the table and pair well with heat.

Experiment with your Flavors!

  • - Always taste as you cook and experiment!

  • - If your food is bland, it might be lacking salt. This can be a delicate balance when trying to have a low sodium diet.

    • More on back -->

  • - Oven roasted peppers like: poblano(ancho), chipotle(mature jalapeno), and habanero can add a lot to a dish without adding salt.

  • - Try to think of recipes as flavor pairings. You might have been missing something all along.

Experiment with your Flavors! Part 2

  • - Season everything!

  • - If you are cooking parts of a recipe seperately be sure to season them before you combine them into the final dish.

    • More on back -->

  • - Be mindful some flavors like sour(acidity) do not hold up well during cooking and will break down in the heat.

  • - Spice(heat) also breaks down while cooking; however, oil spreads spice(heat) while cooking!
